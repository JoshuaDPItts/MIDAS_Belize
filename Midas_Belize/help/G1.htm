G1. Stakeholder Involvement

The level of stakeholder participation in the management of the MMA is a measure of the amount of active involvement of people in making MMA management decisions or participating in management activities. Stakeholders are individuals, groups, or organizations who are interested, involved or affected (positively or negatively) by the MMA. Their actions can support or sustain an MMA, being potential partners or threats in managing the marine reserve. Therefore, the active participation of coastal resource stakeholders in the planning and management of an MMA can improve its success.

Levels of Stakeholder Involvement:

  • Very Low: The level of participation is almost non-existent.
  • Low: Low level of participation may lead to negative impacts.
  • Moderate: Moderate stakeholder participation levels resulting in a slightly higher positive impact for the MMA.
  • High: Involves high level of involvement from a relatively large proportion of stakeholders.
  • Very High: Almost all stakeholders actively participate in management decisions and/or activities.
